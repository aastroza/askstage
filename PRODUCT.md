# Product

## Register

product

## Users

PreguntaYa serves event organizers who need to set up a live Q&A space quickly, and attendees who join from their phones during a talk, panel, class, or meetup. Admins are usually moving fast before or during an event and need a responsive workspace that works on desktop, tablet, and phone. Attendees should be treated as a mobile-first audience: the shared event page is primarily a phone experience and should let them ask, vote, and leave without accounts, instructions, or friction.

## Product Purpose

PreguntaYa makes audience Q&A easy to configure, share, and moderate. Organizers create an event page, share a public link or QR code, and manage questions in one focused workspace. Success means an admin can publish a usable event in minutes, attendees understand what to do immediately, and moderation feels calm even when the room is active.

## Brand Personality

Vivo, simple, facil de usar. The product should feel lively and approachable, with enough polish to be trusted at a real event, but without the stiffness of a corporate admin tool.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS patterns, overly corporate dashboard language, and visual systems that feel cold, bureaucratic, or template-driven. Do not make the product feel like a heavyweight event-management suite. Luma is a useful reference for lightness, confidence, and low-friction event UX, but PreguntaYa should stay focused on live Q&A rather than becoming a marketing surface.

## Design Principles

1. Start from the room: every screen should respect the pressure and momentum of a live event.
2. Remove ceremony: accounts, setup, publishing, sharing, asking, and voting should each feel like the shortest sensible path.
3. Make state obvious: admins should always know what is published, what needs attention, and what attendees will see.
4. Let personality show through clarity: use warm, lively details sparingly, without turning standard controls into puzzles.
5. Design public pages mobile-first: the attendee view should be fastest and clearest on a phone, then scale up gracefully.
6. Keep attendees unblocked: the public event page should explain itself through structure, not instructions.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA for contrast, focus visibility, keyboard access, and readable text. Preserve reduced-motion support for any animated interactions. Public attendee flows must work exceptionally well on small mobile screens, in bright event lighting, and for users who may only have a few seconds to submit or vote. Admin flows should remain fully responsive, but the public event page should be evaluated from a phone viewport first.
